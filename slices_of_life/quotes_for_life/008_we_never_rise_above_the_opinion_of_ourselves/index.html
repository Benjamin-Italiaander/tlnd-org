008 We Never Rise Above The Opinion Of Ourselves

People will never rise above their opinion of themselves

Peter Sage

Peter Sage is making a mindset statement about how your self-image sets the ceiling for your life. Here’s what he means, in simple terms:

⭐ The core idea Link to heading

You cannot consistently behave, choose, or grow in ways that contradict your belief about who you are. If you think you are “not smart,” “not lovable,” “not capable,” or “not worthy,” your life will unconsciously adjust downward to match that belief.

🔍 Why? Link to heading

Because your self-opinion becomes your internal limit:

  • If you believe you don’t deserve good things → you sabotage them.
  • If you see yourself as small → you avoid big opportunities.
  • If you think you’re a failure → you act in ways that confirm it.
  • If you think you’re strong and worthy → you treat yourself better and take bigger steps.

Your brain always tries to keep your life consistent with your identity.

🌱 What he’s encouraging Link to heading

He’s basically saying:

If you want your life to grow, your self-image must grow first.

Not by pretending or faking confidence, but by slowly changing the beliefs you hold about yourself:

  • believing you can learn
  • believing you are worthy
  • believing you can change
  • believing you are allowed to succeed

🧭 A simple example Link to heading

Two people start a course.

Person A: “I’m dumb, I’ll probably fail.” → They study less, feel ashamed, avoid asking questions, and eventually quit.

Person B: “I’m capable of learning this.” → They try, ask help, stay with it, and improve.

Same situation — but their self-opinion decides their outcome.

💬 In short Link to heading

He’s telling you that: Your self-belief is the foundation of every success, relationship, risk, healing, and dream. Raise your opinion of yourself → and your entire life rises with it.
